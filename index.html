Hey! I'm Arjun. If you're looking for my personal Wikipedia hosting, it can be found here. The rest of this page is about my work.

I currently work as a research engineer at the B. John Garrick Institute for the Risk Sciences at the School of Engineering at UCLA. Over the last couple of years, I have served as the software development lead for implementing a suite of tools in the probabilistic risk, reliability, and resilience assessment fields for nuclear, aerospace and power industries. Some of my current projects include HCLA, a system risk and reliability platform, which is used to enhance the design process and assess the reliability of commercial off-the-shelf (COTS) parts in space systems for extended deep space missions at the Jet Propulsion Laboratory (JPL), and Phoenix, a human reliability analysis tool for the Japanese Nuclear Regulation Authority (JNRA).

In the past, I have worked on numerous machine learning projects in the healthcare, bioinformatics and embedded systems domains. One such project is SARP, a smartwatch-based human activity recognition and indoor-positioning platform built at the Center for SMART Health in collaboration with the Embedded and Reconfigurable Systems Lab at UCLA.

In my free time, I contribute as an embedded systems developer to BReATHE, a sensor-based real-time pediatric asthma evaluation platform at the LA PRISMS Center. I'm currently working on renewing my amateur radio license.

If you'd like to collaborate on something interesting, feel free to get in touch!

HCLA - Probabilistic Risk Assessment (PRA) Framework

HCLA is a collaborative web-based tool being developed at UCLA to perform traditional PRA based on the Hybrid Causal Logic (HCL) methodology with advanced time-to-failure models, importance measures, and uncertainty quantification. The HCL methodology employs a model-based approach to system analysis. The framework contains a multi-layer structure that integrates event sequence diagrams (ESDs), fault trees (FTs), and bayesian belief networks (BBNs) without converting the entire system into a large BBN. The models can be created with the IRIS software or directly using XML files.

Phoenix - Human Reliability Assessment (HRA) Framework

Phoenix is a collaborative web-based tool. Built on a cognitive human response model, Phoenix incorporates strong elements of current HRA good practices, leverages lessons learned from empirical studies, and takes advantage of the best features of existing and emerging HRA methods. The methodology introduces the “Crew Response Tree” which provides a structure for capturing the context associated with Human Failure Events (HFEs), including errors of omission and commission. It also uses a team-centered version of the Information, Decision and Action cognitive model and “macro-cognitive” abstractions of crew behavior, as well as relevant findings from cognitive psychology literature and operating experience, to identify potential causes of failures and influencing factors during procedure-driven and knowledge-supported crew-plant interactions.

Deriche-Edges-Hough-Transform

Convex polygon detection using linear hough transform. Polygon detection framework for bitmap images written in C that implements Otsu's thresholding and a Deriche edge detector for blurring, calculation of magnitude and gradient direction, non-maximum suppression and hysteresis thresholding. As a final step, it performs a Hough transform to detect the number and orientation of each polygon edge in the source image.

Indoor-WiFi-Localizer

Indoor localization on an Android device using WiFi 2.4/5GHz, Bluetooth and GSM/UMTS/LTE through supervised learning. Uses a kernelized Naive-Bayes classifier implemented using the Weka machine-learning framework.
